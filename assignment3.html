Using a sensor with an LED!

The assignment: Uses a sensor (e.g. either the photoresistor or the thermistor) in a voltage divider to change the state of an LED

The LED & LDR

In this circuit, a Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) is being used to alter the brightness of an LED. The LED is connected to power through Pin 11 and is set as an OUTPUT, with the other leg connecting to a 1k Ohm resistor and to GND. The Blue LED has a voltage drop of 3.3V. By calculating the resistance required, I found that R = (5V-3.3V)/ 0.02 = 85 Ohms It is because the required amount of resistance for this LED is satisfied by the 1k resistor, that I used it in this circuit. The LDR is connected by one leg to 5V of power, and the other legal to an analog INPUT pin, A0, as well as a 10k Ohm resistor and back to GND. By using the code provided below, the sensor can turn the LED off in the presence of bright light (a certain value in the code) or turn the LED on and vary the amount of brightness.
