Assignment 4: High(er) Voltage and Transistors!

Using a transistor to control load power separate from logic power

The assignment: Uses analogWrite(), a high-load output device, a transistor, and an input sensor

In this circuit, a Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) and a transistor are being used to alter the brightness of an LED strip.

The LDR

The LDR is connected by one leg to 5V of power, and the other leg to an analog INPUT pin, A0, as well as a 10k Ohm resistor and back to GND.

Transistor and LED strip

From the microcontroller, Pin 9 connects to a leg of a 1k Ohm resistor, where the other leg is connected to the Base of the transistor by a wire. The emittor leg of the transistor connects back to ground. The collector leg of the transistor connects to the negative leg of the LED strip through an alligator clip. The positive leg of the LED strip is connected to power by a wire to the positive screw terminal of a 12V power cord, the negative screw terminal connects to the breadboard to circulate back to ground.
